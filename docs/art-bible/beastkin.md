# Darling Blades Art Bible — Beastkin (`bk`)

The Beastkin are the wilds made human-shaped: original beast-girl designs, never
genderbent historicals. Their visual identity is **grounded naturalism** — warm
earth, moss-green, and honest fur naturals (browns, ash-greys, cream, russet)
over the color-identity anchor. Costume language is handcraft, not court: tanned
leathers, bone toggles, woven grass, felted wool, river-worn shell and horn.
Architecture is absent or organic — root-halls, den-mouths, reed shrines, tide
pools — never masonry. Where the token-color pushes (U mermaids, B lamias,
W milkmaids) the accents cool or brighten, but fur, ear, and tail read as **real
animal anatomy on a real woman**, matched to the species sheet below. One key
light, one rim light, hard silhouette; every ear and tail must read at 119×86 px.
